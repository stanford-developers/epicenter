University Innovation Fellows

Fellows pose at the University Innovation Fellows Annual Meetup 2015. Photo by Ben Colman for Epicenter.

About

The University Innovation Fellows program empowers students to become agents of change at their schools. Participants acquire knowledge of tools, frameworks and program models that help college students develop an entrepreneurial mindset and creative confidence. Fellows create new learning opportunities for their peers and inspire them to seize opportunities, define problems and address global challenges. Our Fellows are founding clubs, hosting events and workshops, collaborating with faculty on new classes, creating student makerspaces, and providing opportunities for interdisciplinary collaboration. They serve as advocates for change with academic leaders and represent their schools at national events.

Fellows are sponsored by faculty and administrators at their schools and selected through an application process each spring and fall. After being accepted in to the program, candidates take part in a six-week, online-based experiential training, where they connect with a national network of Fellows, examine their current entrepreneurial ecosystems, formulate action plans, and begin to implement their ideas.

During the Epicenter grant, the University Innovation Fellows program grew and evolved from a prescriptive program to a community of student leaders who discover their own projects and work to create lasting change in higher education.
